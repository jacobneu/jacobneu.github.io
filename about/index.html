About Me

I am currently working as a teaching assistant for the CMU Philosophy Department, and plan to begin my PhD studies in 2021.

My research explores areas of interaction between mathematical logic, philosophy, and computer science, and seeks to leverage these connections to better understand all three. In particular, I'm interested in the philosophy & logic of nondeterministic action, the interaction between topology & logic, and type- & category-theoretic foundations of mathematics (read more about my research here). I also greatly enjoy teaching; my teaching experience encompasses numerous topics across these areas, especially type theory, formal logic, and functional programming (learn more about my teaching).

I was born and raised in Denver, Colorado, and completed my undergraduate (in mathematics) and master's degree (in logic) at Carnegie Mellon University. In addition to my work, I enjoy several hobbies including cooking, camping, reading, and exploring. I also have several other projects I'm working on (or have worked on), see Etc.
